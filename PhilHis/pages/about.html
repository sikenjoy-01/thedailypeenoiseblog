Meet The Team

Nemo Gajes

Spanish Era Contributor

“Writing about the arrival of the Spaniards and the profound changes it ushered in made me realize how pivotal that encounter was for the Philippine archipelago. The merging of indigenous culture with Spanish influences shaped the country’s religious, political, and social structures for centuries. Through my work, I’ve come to appreciate how this era’s complexities continue to influence modern Filipino identity. The articles remind us that history is never simple for it’s a tapestry of encounters, struggles, and adaptations that define a nation’s story.”

Charl Joshua Villanueva

Spanish Era Contributor

“Delving into the complexities of Spanish colonization, especially the social and political upheavals, broadened my understanding of how colonial rule reshaped Filipino society. The narratives of resistance and accommodation highlight the resilience of our ancestors, who navigated foreign dominance while preserving their core values. Writing about these transformative events helped me see history as a living dialogue between the past and present, offering lessons on endurance and cultural synthesis.”

Yzah Rhagene Quintinita

American Era Contributor

“Exploring the American era and its influence on education and governance opened my eyes to a period marked by both progress and conflict. While new institutions were introduced, the fight for true independence was far from over. My articles reflect the tensions between colonial reforms and Filipino aspirations, reminding us that history is layered with hope and hardship. Understanding this era deepens our appreciation of the struggles that paved the way for national identity and self-governance.”

Ian Lloyd Villaro

American Era Contributor

“Writing about the Philippine-American War and the nationalist movements revealed the complexities of a struggle for freedom shadowed by new colonial powers. The resilience of Filipinos during this turbulent period underscores the relentless quest for sovereignty. Through my articles, I aimed to convey how history is a testament to courage and the enduring spirit of a people unwilling to be subdued. It’s a powerful reminder that the path to independence was long and fraught with sacrifice.”

Mark Hilton Dulnuan

Japanese Occupation Contributor

“The Japanese occupation was a dark yet defining chapter in Philippine history. My articles explored life under occupation and the resilience of Filipinos in the face of oppression. It’s essential to remember the hardships endured and the courage displayed by guerrilla fighters and civilians alike. Writing these stories deepened my understanding of sacrifice and the importance of preserving history to honor those who fought for freedom and justice during those years.”

Jervie Martin Akia

Japanese Occupation Contributor

“Covering the guerrilla movements during the Japanese occupation highlighted the bravery and determination of ordinary Filipinos who took extraordinary risks. Their fight was not just against a foreign army but for the soul of the nation. My work reveals how these resistance efforts were crucial in restoring hope and dignity during a time of darkness. This history teaches us that courage often comes in quiet, persistent forms.”

Ken Joshua Infante

Independence Era Contributor

“Researching the road to Philippine independence and the challenges of the Commonwealth period has given me deep appreciation for the complex journey toward nationhood. The resilience shown during post-war recovery illustrated the Filipino spirit's ability to rebuild and aspire for a better future. My articles emphasize that independence was not a single event but a process shaped by political, social, and economic struggles that still resonate today.”

Jesronjohn Quimson

Independence Era Contributor

“Writing about the post-World War II recovery and the eventual independence of the Philippines helped me understand how history is shaped by both resilience and hope. The hardships faced during reconstruction reflect the strength of a nation determined to stand on its own. Through my articles, I wanted to highlight that independence was a collective achievement, built on the sacrifices and perseverance of many Filipinos who refused to lose sight of freedom.”
